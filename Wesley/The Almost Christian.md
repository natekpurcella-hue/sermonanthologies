# The Almost Christian
**Author:** John Wesley

## Introduction
"Almost thou persuadest me to be a Christian." — Acts 26:28

And many there are who go thus far: ever since the Christian religion was in the world, there have been many in every age and nation who were almost persuaded to be Christians. But seeing it avails nothing before God to go only thus far, it highly imports us to consider, first, what is implied in being almost, and secondly, what in being altogether, a Christian.

## I. What is implied in being almost a Christian
**1. Heathen Honesty.**
No one can pretend to be a Christian who has not at least the virtues of a "good heathen." This includes a sense of justice, truth, and humanity.

**2. A Form of Godliness.**
The almost Christian does nothing which the Gospel forbids. He avoids profaning the name of God, idle words, and backbiting. He is temperate and avoids evil.

**3. Doing Good.**
He uses all his power to help others, providing food for the hungry and visiting the sick. He uses the "means of grace"—attending public worship and practicing prayer.

**4. Sincerity.**
A real, inward design to serve God and a hearty desire to do His will. He is not a hypocrite; he truly intends to please God.

## II. What is implied in being altogether a Christian
**1. The Love of God.**
"Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart." This love so engrosses the whole heart that it leaves no room for any affection that does not tend toward God.

**2. The Love of our Neighbor.**
"Thou shalt love thy neighbor as thyself." This includes every human being, even our enemies.

**3. Faith.**
Not merely rational assent, but a "sure trust and confidence that a man hath in God, that, through the merits of Christ, his sins are forgiven."

## III. Application
Is it possible that any of you are not even almost Christians? Even if you have the form of godliness, are you altogether Christians? Do you love God with all your heart?

May we all experience what it is to be not almost only, but altogether Christians! May we be justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in Jesus.
