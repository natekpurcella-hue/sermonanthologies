Time would fail us, if we should attempt to track the Master in his glorious pathway after his resurrection. Let it suffice us briefly to observe that, having led his disciples out unto a mountain, where he has delighted often to commune with them, he was suddenly taken up from them, and a cloud received him out of their sight. We think we may conjecture, by the help of Scripture, what transpired after that cloud had covered him. Did not the angels "Bring his chariot from on high To bear him to his throne, Clap their triumphant wings and cry, His glorious work is done?" Do you not see him, as he mounts his triumphal chariot, "And angels chant the solemn lay, Lift up your heads, ye golden gates, Ye everlasting doors give away?" Behold angels gazing from the battlements of heaven, replying to their comrades who escort the ascending Son of Man.
