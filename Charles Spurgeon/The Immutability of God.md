# The Immutability of God
**Author:** Charles Spurgeon

## Introduction
"I am the Lord, I change not; therefore ye sons of Jacob are not consumed." — Malachi 3:6

It has been said by some one that "the proper study of mankind is man." I will not oppose the idea, but I believe it is equally true that the proper study of God's elect is God; the proper study of a Christian is the Godhead. The highest science, the loftiest speculation, the mightiest philosophy, which can ever engage the attention of a child of God, is the name, the nature, the person, the work, the doings, and the existence of the great God whom he calls his Father.

There is something exceedingly improving to the mind in a contemplation of the Divinity. It is a subject so vast, that all our thoughts are lost in its immensity; so deep, that our pride is drowned in its infinity. Other subjects we can compass and grapple with; in them we feel a kind of self-content, and go our way with the thought, "Behold, I am wise." But when we come to this master-science, finding that our plumb-line cannot sound its depth, and that our eagle eye cannot see its height, we turn away with the thought that vain man would be wise, but he is like a wild ass's colt; and with that solemn exclamation, "I am but of yesterday, and know nothing." No subject of contemplation will tend more to humble the mind, than thoughts of God.

## I. AN UNCHANGING GOD
First of all, we shall consider the doctrine of the **IMMUTABILITY OF GOD**. "I am the Lord, I change not."

**1. God is immutable in his essence.**
His nature did not undergo a change when it became united with the manhood. When Christ in past years did gird himself with mortal clay, the essence of his divinity was not changed; flesh did not become heavenly, nor did the deity become carnal. He did not lose his essence by his incarnation; he remained everlastingly the same. He is not composed of any substance or material, but is spirit—pure, essential, and ethereal spirit—and therefore he is immutable.

**2. He is immutable in his attributes.**
Whatever the attributes of God were of old, that they are now; and of each of them we may say, "I am the Lord, I change not."
- **His power is unchanged.** He is still as mighty as when he spoke the world into existence.
- **His wisdom is unchanged.** He knows as much now as ever, neither more nor less.
- **His justice is unchanged.** Just and holy was he in the past; just and holy is he now.
- **His truth is unchanged.** He has promised, and he brings it to pass.
- **His love is unchanged.** He has not become an almighty tyrant, but his strong love stands like a granite rock.

**3. God is immutable in his plans.**
God is a master-mind; he arranged everything in his gigantic intellect long before he did it; and once having settled it, he never alters it. "This shall be done," saith he, and the iron hand of destiny marks it down.

**4. God is immutable in his promises.**
We are glad to know that there are some things that cannot change. "By two immutable things in which it is impossible for God to lie," he hath signed, confirmed, and sealed every promise of his.

## II. THE PERSONS WHO DERIVE BENEFIT
"Therefore ye sons of Jacob are not consumed." Who are the "sons of Jacob?"

1. **The sons of God's election.** "Jacob have I loved, but Esau have I hated."
2. **Those who have a peculiar interest in the covenant.** Those who have been born again and become sons of God.
3. **Men of peculiar character.** Jacob was a man of prayer. The "sons of Jacob" are wrestling Jacobs, men of prayer.

## III. THE BENEFIT DERIVED
"Therefore ye sons of Jacob are not consumed."

1. **Not consumed by their own sins.** Because He is immutable, He has borne with us.
2. **Not consumed by their trials.** The furnace was hot, but you were not consumed.
3. **Not consumed by their enemies.** The devil and the world have tried, but you are still alive.

## Conclusion
To the believer, it is a source of great comfort. If God has loved you once, He will love you for ever. To the sinner, it is a source of great terror. If God is immutable, then His threats are as sure as His promises. You must be changed, or you can never see His face.
