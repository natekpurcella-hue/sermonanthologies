# "O Lord, revive thy work in the midst of the years, in the midst of the years make known; in wrath remember mercy." — Habakkuk 3:2

**Author:** Charles Finney

## Introduction
"O Lord, revive thy work in the midst of the years, in the midst of the years make known; in wrath remember mercy." — Habakkuk 3:2

A revival is not a miracle. It is a purely philosophical result of the right use of the constituted means—as much so as any other effect produced by the application of means. To expect a revival without using the means is as absurd as for a farmer to expect a harvest without sowing his seed.

## I. What a Revival Is Not
1. **It is not a miracle.** A miracle is something outside the ordinary laws of nature. A revival is not that.
2. **It is not dependent on a miracle.** It follows the ordinary rules of cause and effect.

## II. What a Revival Is
1. **It presupposes a declension.** A revival consists in the return of a church from her backslidings, and in the conversion of sinners.
2. **It includes conviction of sin.** Backslidden professors see their sins in such a light that they often find it impossible to maintain a hope of their acceptance with God.
3. **It includes a return to obedience.** Religion is the work of man. It consists in obeying God with and from the heart.
4. **It includes the conversion of sinners.** When the church is revived, the natural result is the conversion of the ungodly.

## III. The Agencies Employed
1. **The Agency of God.** God is the author of the revival. He provides the truth and sends His Holy Spirit. Without the Spirit, no man would ever obey.
2. **The Agency of Man.** Men are not mere instruments; they are agents. The church must use the means—preaching, prayer, and personal effort.
3. **The Agency of the Sinner.** Conversion consists in the sinner obeying the truth. It is impossible without his cooperation.

## Remarks
Revivals are necessary because men are sluggish. The progress of religion depends on revivals. The church is responsible: if the means are used, the result will follow.
