# The Judgment Seat of Christ
**Author:** Leonard Ravenhill

## Introduction
"O God, stamp eternity upon my eyeballs." — Jonathan Edwards

There are three kinds of reactions to preaching. It makes people sad, mad, or glad. My preaching makes people very glad—they’re glad I’m finishing tonight! Tonight I want to present what I think is the most sobering thing in a Christian’s thought: Judgment.

## I. The Revelation of Jesus Christ
The book of Revelation is a book of mystery, majesty, and misery. It shows the final stage of lost men, that forever they’re going to be cut off from God. If I could transport you out of time into eternity tonight, you’d never be the same. You’d never be the half-backslidden, prayerless folk who like to go to banquets and let others go to hellfire.

## II. The Believer’s Accountability
"For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ." (2 Corinthians 5:10)

1 Corinthians 3 tells us that every man’s work shall be made manifest, for the day shall declare it, because it shall be revealed by fire; and the fire shall try every man’s work of **what sort it is**. Not what size it is. Not the quantity, but the quality.

Dr. A.W. Tozer once said to me, "Len, you know what? We’ll hardly get our feet out of time into eternity and gaze on eternity but we’ll bow our heads in shame and humiliation and say: 'My God, look at all the riches there were in Jesus Christ, and I’ve come to the judgment seat almost a pauper.'"

We are going to stand there alone and be judged for our praying, our giving, our talking, and our doing.

## III. The Great White Throne
Then there is the other side. Revelation 20: "And I saw a great white throne, and him that sat on it... And I saw the dead, small and great, stand before God."

There’s going to be one dramatic moment in history when God is going to put out the lights and pull down the blinds and time has finished. The drunkard, the gambler, the millionaire, and the pauper—none will bribe the Judge of all the earth. Hell is God’s madhouse for those who are morally insane.

## Conclusion
"Only one life, ’twill soon be past, Only what’s done for Christ will last."

Do you think all Christians die happy? Not on your life! Some of them die as miserable as sinners because they’ve misused their time and wasted their lives. Banish our worldliness, Lord. Help us to ever live with eternity’s values in view.
